Rules Changes for 2023-24

Shot Clock Lights (Rule 1-19.4)

Permitted amber lights on the shot clock and an amber strip at the top of the backboard only to signal the end of a shot-clock period.

Uniforms (Rule 1-22.7)

Permitted additional uniform options, including:

  • Identifying names of the institution
  • Allowing more space for logos on the jersey front
  • Allowing numbers 0 or 00 through 99
  • Permitting religious head wear to be worn without a waiver provided it is safe for competition

Shot Clock Reset (Rules 2-11.6.b and 2-11.6.c)

Anytime the ball hits the rim (a pass, deflection or a shot) and the offense retains possession:

  • In frontcourt: Shot clock resets to 20 seconds
  • In backcourt: Shot clock resets to 30 seconds

Legal Guarding Position (Rule 4-17.4.d)

On an airborne player with the ball, the defender must establish legal guarding position before the opponent places the last foot on the floor prior to becoming airborne.

Coach Requested Reviews (Rule 5-14.1)

When a coach requests an out of bounds play to be reviewed under two minutes in the second period and in the last two minutes of any extra period, that team will be charged a timeout if the original call is not overturned.

Flagrant 1 Fouls (Rule 4-12.1)

If a player commits three Flagrant 1 fouls in a game, the player would be disqualified.

Live/Preloaded Video (Rule 10-4.2.c)

Permitted the transmission of live and preloaded video to the bench area for coaching purposes.

Peacekeeper (Rule 10-4.2.j)

Permitted all non-student bench personnel to leave the bench area to assist in preventing a potential fight/altercation.

Goaltending/Basket Interference (Rule 11-2.1.b.6)

Officials may review goaltending/basket interference calls during the next media timeout to ensure accuracy, provided the official makes the call on the floor.

Major Officiating Concerns

The NCAA Men's Basketball Rules Committee issued the following directives to the men's basketball officiating community for the 2023-24 season:

1. Post Play

Call the first displacement foul especially on back down plays. Discern between illegal and incidental contact. When a player dislodges an opponent from a legally established position, it is a foul.

2. Bench Decorum

Consistent enforcement of bench decorum, the coaching box and unsporting conduct rules for both players and bench personnel.

3. Coverage and Whistle Discipline

The consistent and proper implementation of the three-person mechanics system leads to trust and increased call accuracy as a crew. Call accuracy decreases when officials call plays outside their primary coverage area.

4. Traveling

Identify the pivot foot when a player gains control of the ball. When players are allowed to travel, they gain a distinct advantage over their defenders.

5. Clock Awareness

Observe the game clock and shot clock throughout each possession. Obtain the magic number from each position on every possession.

6. Freedom of Movement

Our game is one of movement, screening action and cutters. Be diligent in calling fouls that inhibit players' ability to move freely around the court with or without the basketball.

7. Enforce the Rules

Consistently apply the rules throughout the game.

Rule 1 — Court and Equipment

The Playing Court—Dimensions

The playing court shall be a rectangular surface free from obstructions with:

  • Sidelines: 94 feet in length
  • End lines: 50 feet in length
  • At least 3 feet (preferably 10 feet) of unobstructed space outside sidelines

Three-Point Field-Goal Line

The three-point field-goal line shall be a distance of 22 feet, 1¾ inches from the center of the basket to the outside edge of the line and 21 feet, 7⅞ inches in the corners.

Restricted-Area Arc

The restricted-area arc is a solid single-colored 2-inch line formed by a semicircle measured from the center of the basket to the inside edge with a radius of 4 feet and extending in a straight line to the front face of the backboard.

The Ball

  • Color: Pantone Matching System (PMS) Orange 151, Red-Orange 173 or Brown 1535
  • Circumference: Maximum of 30 inches and minimum of 29½ inches
  • Weight: Not less than 20 ounces nor more than 22 ounces

Uniforms

Numbers: The following numbers are legal: 0-99. Team rosters can include 0 or 00, but not both.

Religious Head Wear: Religious headwear is permitted provided it is securely fastened to the head and is subject to the approval of the referee.

Rule 2 — Officials and Their Duties

The Officials

The officials shall be:

  • A referee and an umpire; or a referee and two umpires
  • Two timers, two scorers and a shot-clock operator

Shot-Clock Operator Duties

Reset to 20 seconds when:

  • The offensive team is the first to gain control in their frontcourt after an unsuccessful field goal attempt that contacts the ring or flange
  • A personal or technical foul is assessed to the defensive team and the ball is to be inbounded in the front court
  • After a try, pass or deflection strikes the ring or flange and the offensive team retains possession in their frontcourt

Reset to 30 seconds when:

  • There is a change in team control and the ball remains live or after a score by the opponent
  • Team control is re-established in the backcourt after the team loses control of the ball

Correctable Errors

The correctable errors are as follows:

  1. Failing to award a merited free throw
  2. Awarding an unmerited free throw
  3. Permitting a wrong player to attempt a free throw
  4. Permitting a player to attempt a free throw at the wrong basket
  5. Erroneously counting or canceling a score

Rule 3 — Players and Substitutes

The Team

At the start of the game, each team shall consist of five players, one of whom shall be the captain.

Substitution

An entering player is a substitute who has been beckoned onto the playing court by an official. A substitute becomes a player when they legally enter the playing court.

Substitutions are NOT permitted:

  • After a successful field goal in the last 59.9 seconds of the second half or any extra period before the clock has been properly started
  • Until the results of an instant replay review have been reported to both head coaches when there is a review in the last two minutes

Rule 4 — Definitions

Airborne Shooter

An airborne shooter is a player who has released the ball on a try for goal. An airborne shooter retains that status until that player has returned to the floor.

Flagrant 1 Personal Foul

A flagrant 1 personal foul is deemed excessive in nature (unwarranted or too much) and/or unnecessary (avoidable, uncalled for or not required by the circumstances of the play), but is not based solely on the severity of the act.

Disqualification: A player who commits three Flagrant 1 fouls in a game shall be disqualified.

Flagrant 2 Personal Foul

A flagrant 2 personal foul involves contact with an opponent that is not only excessive, but also severe (brutal, harsh, cruel) or extreme (dangerous, punishing), while the ball is live. Results in automatic ejection.

Guarding Position

To establish an initial legal guarding position on the player with the ball:

  • The guard shall have both feet touching the playing court
  • The guard's torso shall face the opponent from any direction
  • No time and distance shall be required
  • When the opponent with the ball is airborne, the guard shall have established legal guarding position before the opponent places the last foot on the playing court prior to becoming airborne

Restricted Area

A secondary defender cannot establish initial legal guarding position in the restricted area for the purposes of drawing an offensive foul on a player who is in control of the ball. When illegal contact occurs within this restricted area, such contact shall be called a blocking foul, unless the contact is flagrant.

Rule 5 — Scoring and Timing Regulations

Scoring

  • Two-point goal: A goal from the field, other than from beyond the three-point line
  • Three-point goal: A successful try from beyond the three-point line
  • Free throw: One point

Duration of Play

Playing time for varsity games shall consist of:

  • Two halves of 20 minutes each
  • Halftime intermission: 15 minutes
  • Extra periods: Five minutes each with a one-minute intermission before each

Team Timeouts

In games NOT involving electronic-media timeout format:

  • Four 75-second timeouts
  • Two 30-second timeouts
  • One extra 75-second timeout per extra period

In games involving electronic-media timeout format (at least three per half):

  • Three 30-second timeouts per game (may carry up to two into second half)
  • One 60-second timeout per game
  • One additional 30-second timeout per extra period

Coach Requested Reviews

In the last two minutes of the second period and in the last two minutes of any extra period, when a coach requests an out of bounds play to be reviewed, that team will be charged a timeout if the original call is not overturned.

Rule 6 — Live Ball and Dead Ball

Live Ball

The ball shall become live when:

  • On a jump ball, the ball leaves the official's hand
  • On a throw-in, the ball is at the disposal of the thrower-in
  • On a free throw, the ball is at the disposal of the free-thrower

Alternating-Possession Procedure

The alternating-possession procedure is a method of putting the ball in play with a throw-in rather than a jump ball.

The team that does not obtain control of the initial jump ball shall start the alternating-possession procedure.

Dead Ball

The ball shall become dead or remain dead when:

  • Any goal is made
  • A held ball occurs
  • An official blows the whistle (except when the try is in flight)
  • Time expires for a half or extra period

Rule 7 — Out of Bounds and the Throw-in

Out of Bounds—Player, Ball

A player shall be out of bounds when they touch the floor or any object other than a person on or outside a boundary line.

The ball shall be out of bounds when it touches a player who is out of bounds or any object on or outside a boundary.

Throw-in Requirements

  • The thrower-in shall release the ball not more than five seconds after the throw-in count begins
  • The designated spot shall be 3 feet wide with no depth limitation
  • The thrower-in must keep one foot on or over the designated spot until the ball is released

Rule 8 — Free Throw

Free Throw

A free throw is the privilege given a player to score one point by an unhindered try for goal from within the free-throw semicircle and behind the free-throw line.

Bonus Free Throws

  • Beginning with the 7th team foul: One-and-one (bonus if first is successful)
  • Beginning with the 10th team foul: Two free throws (regardless of whether first is successful)

Free-Throw Requirements

  • The free-thrower shall release the try within 10 seconds
  • The ball must enter the basket or touch the ring or flange
  • Maximum of six players on the lane (four opponents, two teammates)
  • All other players must be behind the free-throw line extended and behind the three-point line

Rule 9 — Violations and Penalties

Major Violations

Traveling

A player shall not travel with the ball. After coming to a stop and establishing the pivot foot:

  • The pivot foot may be lifted, but not returned to the playing court, before the ball is released on a pass or try
  • The pivot foot shall not be lifted before the ball is released to start a dribble

Double Dribble

A player may not dribble a second time after their first dribble has ended.

Three Seconds in the Lane

A player may not remain in the three-second lane for more than three consecutive seconds while their team is in control of the ball in the frontcourt.

10-Second Backcourt

A player or their team shall not be in continuous control of a ball in the backcourt for 10 consecutive seconds.

Shot Clock

The team in control must attempt a try for a field goal within the time allotted (30 seconds). The try must hit the rim or flange, or enter the basket.

Backcourt Violation

A player shall not be the first to touch the ball in the backcourt when the ball came from the frontcourt while that player's team was in team control.

Basket Interference and Goaltending

Basket Interference occurs when a player:

  • Touches the ball while any part of it is within the cylinder
  • Touches the ball or basket while the ball is on or within the basket
  • Causes the basket or backboard to vibrate when the ball is on or within the basket

Goaltending occurs when: A defensive player touches the ball during a field-goal try when the ball is on its downward flight, above the level of the ring, and has the possibility of entering the basket.

Rule 10 — Fouls and Penalties

Personal Fouls

A player shall not hold, displace, push, charge, trip or impede the progress of an opponent by extending arm(s), shoulder(s), hip(s) or knee(s).

Personal Foul Penalties

  • Common foul before bonus: Throw-in to offended team
  • Shooting foul (successful): One free throw plus the made basket
  • Shooting foul (unsuccessful 2-pt): Two free throws
  • Shooting foul (unsuccessful 3-pt): Three free throws
  • Flagrant 1 foul: Two free throws plus possession
  • Flagrant 2 foul: Two free throws plus possession plus ejection

Technical Fouls

Administrative Technical Fouls

Violations of administrative rules (equipment, uniforms, lineups, etc.). One free throw awarded. Does NOT count toward disqualification or ejection.

Class A Technical Fouls

Unsportsmanlike conduct including:

  • Disrespectfully addressing an official
  • Using profanity or vulgarity
  • Taunting or baiting opponents
  • Making obscene gestures

Penalty: Two free throws. Counts toward disqualification/ejection.

Ejection occurs after: Two Class A technical fouls (AA) or one Class A and two Class B technical fouls (ABB)

Class B Technical Fouls

Infractions that do not involve contact or unsportsmanlike conduct, including:

  • Grasping the basket excessively
  • Slapping the backboard
  • Delay of game violations
  • Flopping

Penalty: One free throw. Does NOT count toward team-foul total but counts toward ejection.

Ejection occurs after: Three Class B technical fouls (BBB) or two Class B and one Class A (ABB)

Live/Preloaded Video

Electronic transmission of live statistical data and/or live and preloaded video to the bench area for coaching purposes is now permitted.

Fighting

Any team member or bench personnel who participates in a fight shall be:

  • First offense: Suspended from the team's next regular-season game
  • Second offense: Suspended for the remainder of the season

Rule 11 — Instant Replay

When Instant Replay May Be Used

Mandatory Use

Officials SHALL use replay equipment when there is a reading of zeroes on the game clock at the end of any period to determine:

  • Whether a try for field goal was released before time expired
  • Whether a shot-clock violation occurred before time expired
  • Whether a foul occurred before time expired

Voluntary Use

Officials MAY use replay equipment to:

  • Determine whether a successful try was a two- or three-point attempt
  • Prevent or rectify a scoring mistake
  • Determine if the ball was released on a try before the shot clock expired (only when try is successful)
  • Correct timing mistakes
  • Determine if a flagrant personal foul or flagrant 2 contact technical foul occurred
  • Review goaltending/basket interference calls during the next media timeout (when a call was made on the floor)
  • Determine which team caused the ball to go out of bounds (last 2 minutes only)
  • Determine if a defender was in or out of the Restricted Area (last 2 minutes only)
