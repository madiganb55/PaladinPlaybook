NCAA Softball Rules Guide

2026 and 2027 Edition - Essential Rules and Concepts

The Game Overview

Softball is a game made up of two teams of 9-10 players. The visiting team starts on offense (batting), attempting to score runs by touching all bases including home plate. The home team starts on defense (fielding), attempting to prevent runs and get three outs per inning.

Designated Player (DP) and Flex

Teams may use a 10-player lineup with a Designated Player (DP) and Flex:

  • DP: Bats for the Flex, listed in batting order
  • Flex: Plays defense only, listed in 10th spot
  • Either can substitute for the other with proper reporting
üîÑ Substitution: Starting players may re-enter the game once, returning to their original spot in the batting order.

Pitching Rules

Legal Pitching Position

  • Both feet on ground, pivot foot touching pitcher's plate
  • Hips in line with first and third bases
  • Hands apart when taking signal
  • Hands come together for not more than 5 seconds

Legal Delivery

  • Must take only one step forward toward home plate
  • Pitch must be released underhand, hand below hip
  • Not more than 1¬Ω revolutions of the pitching arm
  • Forward release and follow-through required

Timing

  • Pitcher has 20 seconds to deliver pitch after receiving ball
  • Batter and catcher must be in their boxes within 12 seconds

Batting and Base Running

Strike Zone

The area above home plate between the bottom of the batter's sternum and top of their knees in natural batting stance.

Balls and Strikes

  • Strike: Pitch in strike zone, swung at and missed, or foul ball with less than 2 strikes
  • Ball: Pitch outside strike zone not swung at
  • Base on Balls: Four balls awards first base
  • Strikeout: Three strikes and the batter is out

Dropped Third Strike Rule

With two outs or first base unoccupied and less than two outs, if the catcher fails to catch a third strike before it touches the ground, the batter may attempt to reach first base.

Base Running Basics

  • Runners must touch all bases in order: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, Home
  • May not leave base until pitch is released
  • Must tag up on caught fly balls before advancing
  • Three outs ends the half-inning
üìç Look-Back Rule: When the pitcher has the ball in the circle and the batter-runner has touched first base, runners off base must immediately commit to the next base or return to their previous base without stopping or changing direction.

Special Rules and Situations

Infield Fly Rule

With runners on first and second or bases loaded, and less than two outs, if the batter hits a fair fly ball that can be caught by an infielder with ordinary effort, the batter is automatically out. Runners may advance at their own risk.

Obstruction

Occurs when a defensive player not in possession of the ball or not in the act of fielding impedes a runner's progress. The obstructed runner is awarded bases they would have reached.

Interference

When an offensive player, coach, or equipment denies a fielder a reasonable opportunity to make a play. The interfering player is typically called out.

Eight-Run Rule

A game is called if one team is ahead by eight or more runs after five complete innings (or 4¬Ω if the home team is ahead).

Tiebreaker Rule

If used, starting in extra innings (typically 10th), each team begins its turn at bat with the player scheduled to bat ninth placed on second base.

Game Management

Time Between Innings

Maximum 90 seconds for warmups, beginning when the last defensive player crosses the foul line.

Conferences

  • Each team allowed 7 charged conferences (offensive and defensive combined) per regulation game
  • One additional conference allowed per extra inning
  • Defensive team gets one conference per half-inning, plus one more for each new pitcher

Video Review (if available)

  • Each head coach has 2 challenges per game (retained if call is changed)
  • Crew chief may initiate review beginning in 6th inning
  • Reviewable plays include: fair/foul, home runs, catch/no catch, force/tag plays, interference, obstruction, timing plays
2026/2027 Change: Electronic one-way communication devices now allowed from dugout/coaches' boxes for pitch calling.

Conduct and Safety

Ejections

  • Administrative: Equipment violations, lineup management
  • Behavioral: Unsporting conduct, arguing, inappropriate language
  • Ejected players may remain in dugout but not on field or communicate with umpires
  • Ejected coaches must leave field, dugout, and be out of sight and sound

Safety Protocols

  • Concussions: Player showing signs must be immediately removed and evaluated
  • Lightning: Play suspended when lightning detected in danger zone
  • Collisions: Flagrant/malicious contact results in ejection

Prohibited Actions

  • Using tobacco products on site
  • Intentionally pitching at batters
  • Physical contact with umpires or opponents in confrontational manner
  • Equipment misuse or bringing inappropriate bats onto field
